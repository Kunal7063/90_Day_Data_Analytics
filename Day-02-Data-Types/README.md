# Day 02 – Types of Data

## Structured Data

Structured data is organized in rows and columns, making it easy to store and analyze.

Examples:
- Excel spreadsheets
- SQL databases
- CSV files

Example dataset:

| Customer ID | Name | Age | Purchase Amount |
|-------------|------|-----|----------------|
| 101 | Rahul | 25 | 1200 |
| 102 | Sneha | 31 | 850 |

---

## Unstructured Data

Unstructured data does not follow a fixed format.

Examples include:
- Images
- Videos
- Emails
- Social media posts
- Customer reviews

Example:

Customer review:  
"The product quality is amazing but delivery was late."

---

## Quantitative Data

Quantitative data represents numerical values that can be measured or counted.

Examples:
- Product price
- Number of orders
- Customer age
- Revenue

Example:

| Product | Orders |
|--------|--------|
| Mobile | 320 |
| Laptop | 210 |

---

## Qualitative Data

Qualitative data represents categories or descriptions.

Examples:
- Product category
- Payment method
- Customer gender
- Customer feedback

Example:

| Order ID | Payment Method |
|----------|---------------|
| 101 | UPI |
| 102 | Credit Card |
| 103 | Cash on Delivery |

---

## Reflection

Today I learned the difference between structured and unstructured data, as well as quantitative and qualitative data. Understanding these types helps analysts choose the right tools and analysis methods.

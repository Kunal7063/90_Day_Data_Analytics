Day 01 – Introduction to Data Analytics

📌 What is Data?

    Data is raw facts or information collected for analysis.
    Examples include customer age, product price, sales numbers, website visits, etc.
    
    Raw data alone does not provide value until it is analysed.
📌 What is Data Analytics?

    Data Analytics is the process of:
    
    Collecting data
    
    Cleaning data
    
    Analyzing data
    
    Identifying patterns
    
    Making data-driven decisions
    
    In simple terms, it means converting raw data into meaningful insights.
📌 Types of Data Analytics

    1. Descriptive Analytics
    
    Answers: What happened?
    Example: Total sales last month were ₹5,00,000.

    2. Diagnostic Analytics
    
    Answers: Why did it happen?
    Example: Sales dropped due to reduced website traffic.

    3. Predictive Analytics
    
    Answers: What is likely to happen?
    Example: Sales may increase during the festive season.

    4. Prescriptive Analytics
    
    Answers: What should be done?
    Example: Offer weekend discounts to increase sales.

📌 Role of a Data Analyst

    A Data Analyst:
    
    Collects and cleans data
    
    Uses tools like Excel, SQL, and Python
    
    Creates dashboards and reports
    
    Provides insights to support business decisions
    
    A Data Analyst helps organisations make informed decisions using data.

📌 Reflection

Today, I understood that Data Analytics is not just about charts but about solving business problems using data. I learned the four main types of analytics and how businesses use them for decision-making.


## Practice Example – Flipkart

    ### Data Collected
    - Customer purchase history
    - Product category
    - Order value
    - Delivery location
    - Payment method

    ### Descriptive Analytics
    1. Total electronics sales during the New Year were ₹5 lakh.
    2. The 7-minute grocery delivery service generated ₹9 lakh profit.

    ### Diagnostic Analytics
    Sales dropped during Holi because competing platforms offered free products on purchases above ₹199.

    ### Predictive Analytics
    Mobile phone sales are expected to increase by 70% during the Independence Day sale based on previous trends.

    ### Prescriptive Analytics
    Flipkart could offer discounts and gifts on purchases above ₹299 to attract more customers.
